The Problem Of Western Media

African representation in Western Media has been at the forefront of many tounges for decades. From negative stereotypes such as a "dark nation" (Illinois State) and economic disparities of up to $3.2 billion (The Guardian, 2024) it stands for us to see, react and reform these perceptions and trully explore the question, "Why does this happen and what can we do to stop it?".

What are the Effects?

Tech In Africa

Africa's booming tech sector is a multibillion dollar industry but is shuned by global news outlets with African journalists keying in by echoing western sources (Ajen Africa, 2025). With the most frequent stories used littered with corruption and poverty (Global Media Index, 2024) the tech sector is struggling with startups falling and global measures failing to fit African Innovations (The Conversation, 2025).

The Statistics

The graph on the Left by (The Global Media Index, 2024) is a heatmap showing coverage of the continent by major new outlets. The disparity when it comes to tech and innovation is clear with conflict and famine a clear favorite across all stations. Now after establishing that the problem exists how can we solve it?
