My Vision

The Initiative was built on a single, well defined goal for eqaul representation in the media for all People Moreso those affected by the brunt of stereotypes and prejudice.

I believe that when the world sees Africa clearly — its 54 distinct nations, its billion-person youth surge, its booming technology corridors, its untapped potential — these stereotypes would finnally be abolished and give way to a better world..

The Problem

Western media has spent decades perpetuating stereotypes, misconceptions and a single, tired narrative: poverty, conflict and famine. It is a narrative that is exageratted, has economic effects and dehumanizing.

Studies estimate Africa loses upward of £3.2 billion yearly in inflated borrowing costs alone a direct consequence of how lenders perceive a continent they were never taught to see truthfully. The media problem is also a money problem.

My Journey

How this became real

The Spark

The Question Nobody Could Answer

It started with a project — To chose a topic i affiliate with and explore in depth research I noticed that every time Africa came up in global contexts, the stories were one and the same. Corruption - Poverty - Diseases.The African innovations , especially in tech, were pushed to the side.

The Research

Following the Data

I spent a month finding data to bring to light if the perceptions were real or misconcieved. Exploring data from The Guardian, Global Media Index and The Big Stack I finally came to the conclusion about growth. A booming tech sector with hubs such as Kigali to name one. The world's youngest population(Maybe due to low life expectancy?). Cities growing faster than anywhere on earth.

The Build

Turning Research Into Something Real

I used my previous knowledge in flask and html,css and js to build a website where the data can be presented as what it is with my own views on what needs to change and how I think it could occur in the world as we know it.
