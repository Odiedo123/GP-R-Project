My Vision

The Odiedo Initiative was built on a single conviction: that African people deserve equal, accurate, and dignified representation in global media and economic conversation. Not charity. Not pity. Representation on equal terms.

I believe that when the world sees Africa clearly — its 54 distinct nations, its billion-person youth surge, its booming technology corridors, its ancient wisdom traditions — the conversation about the continent changes permanently.

The Problem

Western media has spent decades collapsing a continent of 1.4 billion people into a single, tired narrative: poverty, conflict, disease. It is a narrative that is statistically false, economically damaging, and humanly insulting.

Studies estimate Africa loses upward of £3.2 billion yearly in inflated borrowing costs alone — a direct consequence of how lenders perceive a continent they were never taught to see truthfully. The media problem is also a money problem. And most importantly, it is a dignity problem.

My Journey

How this became real

The Spark

The Question Nobody Could Answer

It started with a classroom conversation — or rather, the absence of one. I noticed that every time Africa came up in global contexts, the same images appeared. Wars. Famine. Aid workers. I started asking: where are the engineers? The architects? The founders? Nobody had a good answer.

The Research

Following the Data

I spent months in the World Happiness Report, African Development Bank publications, and UN datasets. The numbers were astonishing — not for what they showed about poverty, but for what they showed about growth. A booming fintech sector. The world's youngest population. Cities growing faster than anywhere on earth.

The Build

Turning Research Into Something Real

I taught myself Flask, data visualisation, and web design to build a platform that could hold this evidence. Every section of this site — the happiness data, the country showcase, the media analysis — came from real published research, not opinion. The goal was always: show the data, let it speak.

Now

The Odiedo Initiative Goes Live

This site is a living document. Every section will grow. Every dataset will be updated. The connect page links to every source so you can verify everything yourself. This is not advocacy dressed as journalism — it is journalism built on data, offered freely to anyone willing to look.
